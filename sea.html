About the Sea

Where Unfinished Stories Come Ashore

The Sea is the museum’s most intimate space. Here, lost things have not yet become formal exhibits. They arrive as fragments, feelings, and unfinished stories.

Some remain here like bottles floating near the tide. Others may one day be selected and transformed into exhibits in the Gallery.

What Belongs Here?

Objects, words, places, memories

A lost thing does not have to be physical. It may be something once held, written, visited, or deeply remembered.

Categories of Loss

What the Sea Receives

You may submit a physical item, a piece of writing, a place you can no longer return to, or a memory that still lingers.

Lost Objects

Physical things

Keys, jewelry, toys, clothes, tickets, gifts, and other objects once held in daily life.

Lost Words

Text and messages

Letters, notes, unsent drafts, messages, and fragments of language that still carry emotion.

Lost Places

Locations once known

A street, a room, a hometown corner, or any place that now exists mainly in memory.

Lost Memories

Feelings and moments

A season of life, a relationship, a brief encounter, or a moment that cannot be physically kept.

How to Contribute

How to Send a Bottle into the Sea

Share a thing, memory, word, or place you once held and lost. Your entry will become part of the drifting collection.

1. Choose What Was Lost

Decide whether your entry belongs to Objects, Words, Places, or Memories.

2. Give It a Title

Use a short title that is clear, poetic, or personal.

3. Tell the Story

Briefly explain what was lost, how it disappeared, or why it still matters.

4. Keep It Gentle and Respectful

Write with care. Avoid private information you do not want to make public.

Submission Form
