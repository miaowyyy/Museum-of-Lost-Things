What This Website Is

A Museum for Small, Ordinary Forms of Loss

The Museum of Lost Things imagines loss not only as the disappearance of objects, but also as the fading of places, relationships, habits, capacities, and periods of life. It turns those quiet absences into something visitors can read, feel, and reflect on.

Rather than functioning as a traditional archive, the site sits between exhibition, personal essay, and interactive submission space. It is meant to feel gentle and contemplative: part shoreline, part notebook, part curatorial record.

Core Idea

Not everything lost is dramatic.

Some losses are soft: a former ease, a childhood room, a way of seeing, a sentence not said in time. This project gives those quieter losses a visual form.

  • Objects can become records.
  • Private feeling can become shared reflection.
  • Interface can carry emotion, not only function.

Aim and Purpose

What the Project Tries to Do

The goal is not simply to display content. It is to build a digital space where narrative, interface, and atmosphere work together to make everyday loss feel legible and meaningful.

Aim 01

Archive Everyday Loss

To create a place for ordinary things that are often too small, too personal, or too emotionally ambiguous to fit into formal archives.

Aim 02

Translate Emotion into Interface

To use visual language, pacing, typography, and interaction to communicate tenderness, distance, drift, and recollection rather than relying only on text.

Aim 03

Invite Reflection and Contribution

To encourage visitors to think about what they have lost and, if they choose, let their own story enter the shared sea of memory.

Site Structure

How Visitors Move Through the Museum

The site is organized as a gentle sequence rather than a menu of disconnected pages. The home page introduces the concept, The Sea receives submissions and drifting stories, and the Gallery becomes the more curated, archival layer of the project.

This structure supports the core idea that a private loss first arrives as something fluid, uncertain, and personal before it is re-framed as part of a collective museum.

The Sea

A threshold for submission

Visitors can browse categories of loss, read the submission guide, and send a story into the sea. This page emphasizes intimacy, drift, and participation.

The Gallery

A curated archival layer

Selected stories and objects appear as recovered records. The interaction becomes quieter and more formal, as though memory has been catalogued without losing its emotional texture.

Why It Matters

A small digital space for noticing what disappears

This project is ultimately about attention: to the objects we outgrow, the places we avoid, the memories that blur, and the emotional weight carried by ordinary things.

It asks whether a website can function not only as information architecture, but also as a gentle emotional environment.
